ASTM B117 Salt Spray Testing — Acceptance Criteria and Corrosion Evaluation

ASTM B117 is the internationally recognized standard for salt spray testing, used to evaluate the corrosion resistance of metal surfaces and protective coatings. The test exposes a metal specimen to a continuous salt fog environment at a controlled temperature of 35 degrees Celsius using a five percent sodium chloride solution with a pH between 6.5 and 7.2.

The purpose of salt spray testing is to provide an accelerated, reproducible method of evaluating corrosion resistance. It does not perfectly replicate outdoor weathering, but it provides a reliable comparative measure of how materials perform under corrosive conditions.

For uncoated steel surfaces, the appearance of red rust during salt spray testing is considered a failure. The time to first rust appearance is used as a measure of the material's corrosion resistance. Uncoated carbon steel typically shows rust within 24 hours of exposure. Zinc-plated steel is expected to show no base metal red rust for a minimum of 72 hours under ASTM B117 conditions.

For coated surfaces, failure criteria include visible blistering of the coating, red rust breakthrough to the substrate, adhesion loss at scribe lines, and underfilm corrosion extending more than one millimeter from any scribed area. A coated surface that shows any of these defects within the specified test duration is considered to have failed the standard.

High-performance industrial coatings are typically expected to withstand between 500 and 1000 hours of salt spray exposure without failure. Marine-grade coatings and specialized industrial coatings may be required to withstand over 2000 hours without visible corrosion.

A component that has passed ASTM B117 testing at the required duration for its application class is considered acceptable for continued service in its target environment. A component that fails ASTM B117 testing requires either recoating, surface treatment, or replacement depending on the severity and location of the failure.
